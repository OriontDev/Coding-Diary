God defend New Zealand


"God Defend New Zealand" was written in 1876 by poet Thomas Bracken and set to music by John Joseph Woods, who composed the melody in response to a competition. Originally a patriotic hymn, it gained popularity over the years and was frequently performed at public events. In 1940, it was prominently featured during the centenary of the Treaty of Waitangi. It officially became one of New Zealand's national anthems in 1977, alongside "God Save the King," symbolizing the nation's cultural identity and independence.
